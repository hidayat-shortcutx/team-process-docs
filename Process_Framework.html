How We Work — Process Framework

This document captures how the team operates across all projects and campaigns: the five stages every initiative passes through, who owns what at each stage, the key approval gates, and how it all plays out in a concrete monthly example.

Roles: CEO Head of Marketing Brand Manager Digital Marketing Manager eCommerce Manager eCommerce Executive Content Creator Videographer Media Manager  (faded = doc coming soon)

The 5-Stage Project Lifecycle

Every campaign, launch, or initiative — regardless of size — moves through these five stages. The Head of Marketing leads from the top — all roles report in to them, except the CEO. The Brand Manager is their right hand: the central connective tissue for day-to-day creative and operational management, involved in almost every stage.

  • Head of Marketing sets marketing strategy & quarterly creative direction
  • Head of Marketing conceptualises the campaign with Brand Manager: concept, messaging, story
  • Head of Marketing aligns concept with CEO; Brand Manager supports
Campaign concept + quarterly creative direction
Stage 2
Plan
Brand Manager eCommerce Manager Digital Marketing Manager
  • Brand Manager plans campaign: timeline, channels, deliverables
  • Brand Manager issues brief to all executing teams
  • eCommerce Manager plans quarterly calendar, registers platforms promos
  • eCommerce Manager briefs Digital Marketing Manager + Media Manager
  • Digital Marketing Manager plans EDM lineup & segmentation
Campaign brief issued · Calendar locked · Promos registered
Stage 3
Create

  • Videographer: brand films, product videos, motion graphics
  • Brand Manager: 30 static ad assets (10 per TOFU/MOFU/BOFU)
  • Digital Marketing Manager: EGC/UGC videos, EDM drafts, Shopify banner

Approval Gates

Nothing goes live without clearing the relevant gate. The Brand Manager is the primary day-to-day approval authority for creative and operational outputs. Strategic and brand-level decisions are approved by the Head of Marketing, who reports to the CEO.

eCommerce campaign calendar & mechanics eCommerce Manager Brand Manager Presentation / Google Doc 1 week turnaround · Planned 1 quarter ahead

Key Collaboration Flows

These are the recurring cross-role touchpoints that keep everything connected — not one-off handoffs but standing interactions that run on a defined cadence.

Media Manager ↔ eComm Manager ↔ HoM

  • Weekly performance sync — Media Manager, eCommerce Managers, and Head of Marketing review paid channel performance in Google Data Studio; creative fatigue signals and optimisation priorities are identified and agreed
  • Creative handoff — DMM uploads approved ad creative (EGC + UGC videos) to Google Shared Drive; Media Manager uploads to platforms and manages spend
  • Creative refresh brief — eCommerce Manager briefs DMM on refresh needs within 24 hours of the weekly sync; DMM produces new creative against the agreed timeline
  • Monthly creative refresh — triggered by the eCommerce Manager following the sync or by Media Manager flagging fatigue

Head of Marketing ↔ CEO

  • Fortnightly strategic check-in — Head of Marketing shares brand health, campaign performance, competitor insights, and growth recommendations; strategic decisions are made together
  • Quarterly direction-setting — CEO and Head of Marketing align on creative priorities and brand vision for each brand across the portfolio
  • Post-campaign review — Head of Marketing presents consolidated results to CEO within 1 week of campaign end

Head of Marketing ↔ Brand Manager

  • Right-hand relationship — Brand Manager is the Head of Marketing's primary operational partner; involved in almost every campaign decision, from brief to post-campaign review
  • Standing alignment — Head of Marketing delegates day-to-day creative and team management to Brand Manager; Brand Manager escalates blockers, risks, and strategic questions upward
  • Brief & review handoff — Head of Marketing sets the strategic direction; Brand Manager translates this into actionable briefs and manages execution quality across the team

eCommerce Manager ↔ DMM + Media Manager

  • Quarterly campaign brief — eCommerce Manager shares the upcoming quarter's campaign calendar and mechanics with DMM (for creative production lead time) and Media Manager (via a call, to align on paid media asset requirements)
  • Ongoing: creative readiness — if creative isn't ready in time for platform setup, eCommerce Manager flags to DMM and Brand Manager immediately

Brand Manager ↔ Content Creator + Videographer

  • Per campaign / monthly — Brand Manager issues production brief with visual references, creative direction, specs, and deadlines
  • Milestone check-ins — Brand Manager reviews at concept/storyboard, rough cut, and final stages — not just at the end
  • Weekly team check-in — Brand Manager runs a standing team check-in to surface blockers and maintain alignment

eCommerce Manager ↔ eCommerce Executive

  • Campaign setup — eCommerce Manager leads platform setup (pricing, mechanics, pages); eCommerce Executive assists on request and owns live streaming execution
  • Daily platform monitoring — eCommerce Manager monitors listing health, pricing, and ad budget; eCommerce Executive flags any issues they spot in-platform
  • Live streaming — eCommerce Executive runs sessions independently; eCommerce Manager tracks GMV performance

DMM ↔ Content Creator + Videographer

  • Monthly EGC production — DMM produces 9 EGC videos per month in collaboration with Brand Manager and Content Creator
  • UGC / KOL videos — DMM works with eCommerce Executive to approve KOLs and reviews submitted UGC (3 videos/month)
  • Motion graphics — DMM discusses execution needs with Videographer for any animated or motion content

Monthly Campaign: Step by Step

A concrete walkthrough of how a monthly cross-channel promotional campaign runs — from first idea to post-campaign review. Campaign launches in Week 1 of Month X; all preparation happens in Month X-1.

APPROVAL GATE marks a step that cannot be skipped — nothing advances without this sign-off.  TOOL marks the system used for that step.  DEADLINE marks a hard timing constraint.
Month X-1 · Week 1–2 Stage 1–2: Strategise & Plan ▶
CEO Shares monthly business priorities and revenue targets with Head of Marketing — the foundation every campaign is built from.
Head of Marketing Conceptualises the campaign — defines the creative concept, key messaging, hero visual direction, and the story the campaign will tell. Developed with Brand Manager and aligned with CEO. APPROVAL GATE Campaign concept must be CEO-aligned before the brief goes out.
Brand Manager Issues campaign brief to all teams — concept, timeline, deliverables, brand standards, and success criteria. DEADLINE Brief must be issued at least 2 weeks before the earliest asset deadline. TOOL ClickUp or Google Drive
eCommerce Manager Submits campaign calendar and mechanics for Brand Manager approval — platform registrations (Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, Lazada), discount mechanics, GWP sourcing flag. APPROVAL GATE Brand Manager approval required before any platform registration or setup proceeds. Target: 1 week turnaround.
eCommerce Manager Briefs Digital Marketing Manager on campaign creative needs (for production lead time) and calls Media Manager to align on required paid media assets.
Digital Marketing Manager Plans the monthly EDM lineup — determines which sends (per tier A/B/C/D), which segments, and which send dates align with the campaign.
Month X-1 · Week 2–3 Stage 3: Create ▶
Content Creator Produces social content per brief — reels, carousels, stories, TikToks — with visual references and caption direction from Brand Manager. Brand Manager checks in at concept and draft stage.
Videographer Shoots and edits video assets per brief — brand film, product video, motion graphics. Milestone reviews at storyboard and rough cut stage with Brand Manager.
Brand Manager Produces static ad creative batch — 30 assets total (10 per funnel stage: TOFU, MOFU, BOFU) as part of the hyper-personalised ad strategy.
Digital Marketing Manager Produces EGC videos (9/month, in collaboration with Brand Manager & Content Creator) and UGC videos (3/month, via eCommerce Executive and shortlisted KOLs). Also designs EDM drafts in Klaviyo and the Shopify homepage banner.
Digital Marketing Manager Submits EDM lineup, segmentation rules, and drafts to Brand Manager for approval. APPROVAL GATE Brand Manager must approve before any EDM is scheduled or sent. DEADLINE Must be submitted and approved by 3rd week of Month X-1. TOOL Google Doc → Google Drive folder
Brand Manager Reviews ALL submitted assets — checks creative quality, brand guideline compliance, and strategic alignment with the campaign concept. Approves or returns with specific feedback. 2 business day turnaround per review batch. No asset goes live without Brand Manager clearance.
eCommerce Manager Submits platform promo registrations (after Brand Manager approval on calendar) and begins configuring campaign mechanics on each platform — pricing, bundles, campaign pages.
Month X-1 · Week 4 Pre-Launch: Final Setup ▶
Digital Marketing Manager Submits Shopify banner for Brand Manager approval via ClickUp. APPROVAL GATE ClickUp card created, Brand Manager must approve before publishing. DEADLINE Submitted at least 3 days before go-live. 1 business day turnaround.
Brand Manager Approves final ad creative and uploads to Google Shared Drive — static assets (30) handed off to Media Manager for paid ads. Soft approval on DMM's video creative batch. APPROVAL GATE Media Manager cannot upload creative to platforms until it has been approved by the Head of Marketing and uploaded to the Google Shared Drive.
Media Manager Sets up paid ad campaigns in Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, and TikTok Ads using the approved creative batch. Configures targeting, budgets, and scheduling for campaign go-live. TOOL Meta / Google / TikTok Ads · Google Data Studio for reporting
Digital Marketing Manager Schedules approved EDMs in Klaviyo — per segmentation rules and planned send dates. Sends test sends to confirm rendering, links, and personalisation before the live send. TOOL Klaviyo
eCommerce Manager Completes and QAs platform campaign setup across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada — pricing, discounts, bundles, and campaign pages confirmed accurate.
eCommerce Executive Confirms live streaming schedule and briefs KOLs on posting dates, content requirements, and campaign messaging ahead of launch week.
Month X · Week 1 Stage 4: Execute — Launch Week ▶
Digital Marketing Manager Shopify homepage banner goes live — published and QA'd (desktop + mobile rendering, links, load time).
Digital Marketing Manager EDMs send per schedule to correct segments in Klaviyo. Social content publishes per the content calendar across Instagram and TikTok.
Media Manager Paid ads activate across Meta, Google, and TikTok. Media Manager monitors daily — spend pacing, delivery, early performance signals.
eCommerce Executive Live streaming sessions run on TikTok Shop, Shopee Live, and Lazada Live. KOLs post their campaign content.
eCommerce Manager Monitors platforms throughout launch week — listing health, price accuracy, promo mechanics, and ad budget pacing. Flags and resolves issues immediately. Daily check: sufficient budget for GMV Products and GMV Live ads to run continuously.
Month X · Week 2–3 During Campaign: Monitor & Optimise ▶
Media Manager eComm Manager Head of Marketing Weekly performance sync — Media Manager, eCommerce Managers, and Head of Marketing review paid channel performance in Google Data Studio. Creative fatigue signals are flagged; eCommerce Manager briefs DMM on refresh needs post-sync. TOOL Google Data Studio · Scheduled weekly meeting
Media Manager Adjusts ad spend and targeting based on performance signals. Any budget changes require Head of Marketing approval before execution. APPROVAL GATE Budget changes → Head of Marketing must approve before Media Manager acts.
Digital Marketing Manager Monitors EDM and social performance in Klaviyo and platform analytics. Flags notable drops or spikes to Brand Manager with proposed fixes.
eCommerce Manager Tracks platform GMV, conversion, and campaign uplift daily. Continues monitoring listing health and promo mechanics throughout the campaign window.
Post-Campaign Stage 5: Measure — Review & Learn ▶
Media Manager Produces ad performance report — impressions, clicks, ROAS, total spend breakdown by platform and funnel stage. TOOL Google Data Studio
Digital Marketing Manager Reports EDM, social, and website performance — open rates, click rates, revenue attribution (Klaviyo), social engagement, website traffic uplift.
eCommerce Manager Reports platform performance — GMV by platform, campaign uplift vs. baseline, conversion rate, live streaming contribution.
Brand Manager Synthesises all reports into a post-campaign review — what worked, what didn't, why, and specific recommendations for the next cycle. DEADLINE Completed and submitted to Head of Marketing within 1 week of campaign end.
Head of Marketing Reviews the post-campaign report with Brand Manager — validates findings, adds strategic context, and prepares a consolidated summary to bring to the CEO.
CEO Reviews consolidated post-campaign summary with Head of Marketing. Insights and decisions feed directly into the next campaign's Strategise stage — closing the loop.
