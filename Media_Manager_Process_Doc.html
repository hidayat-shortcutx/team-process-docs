Purpose

This process ensures the team's paid media investment is directed intentionally — with a quarterly topline strategy setting channel allocation, funnel mapping, and budget split, supported by a monthly tactical review that tracks performance against targets and adjusts where needed. Unit economics are run against proposed campaign metrics before any plan goes to the Head of Marketing for sign-off, ensuring every campaign is commercially viable before spend begins.

Scope

In Scope

  • Quarterly topline paid media strategy: channel selection, funnel stage mapping (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU), and budget allocation across Meta, Google, and TikTok
  • Monthly tactical review: performance vs. quarterly targets, channel mix assessment, and pacing adjustments within the approved budget
  • Unit economics analysis per proposed campaign: AOV, CAC, OpEx, pricing, and profitability assessment — completed before the plan is submitted to the Head of Marketing
  • Campaign structure planning: objective, targeting approach, ad formats per channel
  • Quarterly strategy and monthly review presentations to Head of Marketing for sign-off
  • Briefing the eCommerce Manager on the approved plan
  • Maintaining an always-on media plan document in Google Drive

Out of Scope

  • Creative production — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Organic social media strategy — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Campaign-specific budget uplifts for eCommerce campaigns (covered in Section 3)
  • Setting overall marketing budget — determined by the Head of Marketing and CEO

Process Flow

  1. Pull the previous quarter's (or month's) performance data from Google Data Studio — ROAS, CTR, CPM, CPC, and conversion rate per channel.
  2. Identify which channels performed above/below target and carry insights into the new plan.
  3. Draft the media plan: assign each channel a funnel role (TOFU / MOFU / BOFU), set campaign objectives, and propose budget split. For quarterly plans this is a full topline strategy; for monthly reviews this is a tactical adjustment within the approved quarterly framework.
  4. Consult with the Digital Marketing Manager to confirm the plan maps to the creative pipeline and what ad formats and volumes are achievable.
  5. Run unit economics for each proposed campaign: model AOV, projected CAC, OpEx, and expected ROAS to confirm the campaign is profitable at the proposed spend level. Consult the eCommerce Manager for current pricing and mechanics. Do not submit the plan to HoM if the unit economics do not support the proposed budget.
  6. Present the plan and unit economics summary to the Head of Marketing for review and sign-off. No new spending begins without this approval.
  7. Incorporate any feedback, finalise the plan, and publish it to the shared Google Drive folder.
  8. Brief the eCommerce Manager on media timing and campaign windows so platform setups are aligned.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Review Previous Performance ►
Owner: Media Manager
When: First week of each new quarter (full strategy); first week of each month (tactical review)

Open Google Data Studio and pull ROAS, CTR, CPM, CPC, and conversion rate for the prior period, broken down by channel (Meta, Google, TikTok) and campaign type (TOFU, MOFU, BOFU). Note which channels and ad formats outperformed or underperformed targets. For monthly reviews, assess performance against the quarterly targets set in the approved plan and identify any pacing issues or channel adjustments needed within the existing budget.

Step 2: Draft the Media Plan ►
Owner: Media Manager
Consult: Digital Marketing Manager

For quarterly plans, draft the full topline strategy covering:

  • Channel allocation — which percentage of budget goes to Meta, Google, TikTok
  • Funnel mapping — which channel and ad format serves TOFU (awareness), MOFU (consideration), BOFU (conversion)
  • Campaign objectives — reach, traffic, conversion, retargeting — per channel
  • Ad formats — video, carousel, static, search, performance max, etc.
  • Targeting approach — audiences, lookalikes, remarketing lists

For monthly reviews, document proposed tactical adjustments: channel reallocation, budget pacing changes, or new campaign types within the approved quarterly framework. Consult with the Digital Marketing Manager to confirm the creative pipeline supports the planned mix.

Step 3: Run Unit Economics per Proposed Campaign ►
Owner: Media Manager
Consult: eCommerce Manager (pricing & campaign mechanics)

Before submitting any plan to the Head of Marketing, run unit economics for each proposed campaign to confirm it is commercially viable. The analysis must cover:

  • AOV (Average Order Value) — expected average order value based on the campaign's product mix, bundle, or voucher mechanics
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) — projected cost to acquire one customer at the proposed budget and conversion rate targets
  • OpEx — relevant operating costs that affect the margin available per order (fulfilment, platform fees, etc.)
  • Pricing & profitability — confirm that after CAC and OpEx, the campaign generates an acceptable margin; flag if not
  • ROAS target — back-calculate the minimum ROAS required for the campaign to break even, and confirm the proposed budget is achievable at that threshold

Consult the eCommerce Manager for the current pricing, voucher mechanics, and bundle details for each campaign being modelled. Do not submit the plan to the Head of Marketing if the unit economics show the campaign is loss-making at the proposed spend level — either revise the budget, mechanics, or escalate to HoM with a clear explanation of the trade-off.

Step 4: Present to Head of Marketing for Sign-Off ►
Owner: Media Manager
Accountable: Head of Marketing

Schedule a review with the Head of Marketing. Present the plan with performance context, channel rationale, proposed budget split, and the unit economics summary for each campaign. Capture any changes or directives from the HoM.

No new spending or material reallocation begins until this approval is given. If the meeting is delayed, the existing plan continues at maintenance level until the new plan is signed off.
Step 5: Finalise, Publish & Brief Stakeholders ►
Owner: Media Manager

Incorporate HoM feedback, finalise the media plan document, and upload it to the shared Google Drive folder. Brief the eCommerce Manager specifically on media timing around campaign windows (6.6, 9.9, 11.11, 12.12, seasonal events) so platform setups and paid amplification are coordinated.

Exceptions & Edge Cases

SituationAction
HoM is unavailable for the quarterly strategy review or monthly reviewContinue with the current approved plan at maintenance spend. Do not reallocate budget or start new campaign types without sign-off. Reschedule within 2 weeks for quarterly; within 1 week for monthly.
Unit economics show a proposed campaign is loss-makingDo not submit to HoM as-is. Revise the budget or mechanics first, or escalate to HoM with the full unit economics breakdown and a clear explanation of the trade-off and proposed options.
A new channel (e.g. Pinterest, YouTube) is proposed mid-quarterEscalate to HoM for approval before any test budget is allocated. Document the rationale, present as a mini-strategy brief, and include unit economics for the proposed test.
Creative pipeline is delayed and cannot support the planned channel mixFlag to HoM and DMM immediately. Temporarily shift budget to channels where existing creative is available, and update the media plan.

Purpose

This process ensures always-on paid campaigns across Meta, Google, and TikTok run continuously, are set up correctly, are refreshed with approved creative before fatigue sets in, and are monitored daily so performance issues are caught and acted on quickly.

Scope

In Scope

  • Receiving approved creative assets from the Digital Marketing Manager via Google Shared Drive
  • Uploading and configuring ad sets on Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, and TikTok Ads Manager
  • Setting targeting parameters, bid strategies, and daily/lifetime budgets
  • Daily monitoring of CTR, ROAS, CPM, CPC, frequency, and conversion rate
  • Flagging creative fatigue to the Digital Marketing Manager when performance signals indicate a refresh is needed
  • Optimising bids, budgets, and targeting within the approved media plan

Out of Scope

  • Creating ad creative — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Content calendar and organic post scheduling — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Setting the overall campaign budget — set at strategy stage (Section 1) or via HoM approval for changes (Section 4)
  • Campaign-specific eCommerce campaign pushes (6.6, 9.9, etc.) — covered in Section 3

Process Flow

  1. Digital Marketing Manager uploads approved creative to the Google Shared Drive folder and notifies the Media Manager.
  2. Media Manager downloads assets, checks specs (dimensions, file size, copy length) against each platform's requirements, and flags any issues to the DMM before uploading.
  3. Upload creative to Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, and/or TikTok Ads Manager. Set campaign objective, targeting, ad set budget, bid strategy, and scheduling.
  4. Once live, monitor daily — pull CTR, ROAS, CPM, CPC, frequency, and conversion rate from each platform.
  5. If any creative shows a consistent decline in CTR or frequency above 3 over two consecutive weeks, trigger a formal creative refresh request to the DMM (see Section 6: Weekly Creative Performance Sync).
  6. Optimise active campaigns continuously: adjust bids, refine targeting, pause underperforming ad sets, and reallocate budget within the approved plan.

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Receive Creative Assets from DMM ►
Handoff from: Digital Marketing Manager

The DMM places final approved creative into the designated Google Shared Drive folder and notifies the Media Manager (via ClickUp or agreed channel). The MM must not upload any creative that has not been approved through the DMM's own sign-off process.

Owner: Media Manager

Before uploading to any platform, verify that each asset meets platform spec requirements — image dimensions, video length, file size, aspect ratio, and text overlay limits. If an asset fails a spec check, return it to the DMM with a clear note on what needs to be fixed. Do not upload non-compliant assets.

Step 3: Upload, Configure & Launch ►
Owner: Media Manager

Upload the creative to each applicable platform (Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, TikTok Ads Manager). For each campaign, configure:

  • Campaign objective — aligned to funnel stage in the approved media plan
  • Targeting — audience segments, lookalikes, or remarketing lists as per the plan
  • Bid strategy — lowest cost, target CPA, or ROAS-based as appropriate
  • Daily/lifetime budget — within the approved allocation
  • Scheduling — start date, end date (or ongoing for always-on)
Step 4: Daily Performance Monitoring ►
Owner: Media Manager
Frequency: Every business day

Pull daily metrics from each platform for all active campaigns: CTR, ROAS, CPM, CPC, frequency, and conversion rate. Log results in the tracking sheet or Google Data Studio. Any metric that moves 20% or more from the rolling weekly average should be flagged for investigation that same day.

Step 5: Optimise Active Campaigns ►
Owner: Media Manager

Based on daily monitoring, optimise continuously. Allowable optimisations within the approved plan (no HoM approval needed):

  • Adjusting bids within the same campaign
  • Pausing underperforming ad sets
  • Shifting budget between ad sets within the same campaign
  • Refining audience targeting within the approved strategy

Any change to total campaign budget or channel allocation requires HoM approval first — see Section 4: Budget Management.

Creative is delayed and campaigns have no fresh assetsFlag to HoM and DMM. Keep existing creative running if still within acceptable frequency. If frequency is already high, reduce budget on that ad set and escalate to HoM.
Platform algorithm change causes sudden performance dropDocument the change, flag to HoM and BM with context, and propose a bid/targeting adjustment. Do not reallocate budget across channels without HoM approval.

Purpose

This process covers the Media Manager's role in paid media amplification during eCommerce campaign windows — 6.6, 9.9, 11.11, 12.12, Raya, CNY, and any other campaign period designated by the brand calendar. The goal is to coordinate paid amplification with the eCommerce Manager's platform mechanics, so spend, targeting, and timing are fully aligned before each campaign goes live.

Scope

In Scope

  • Campaign brief intake from Head of Marketing ahead of each eCommerce campaign window
  • Coordinating with eCommerce Manager on campaign mechanics (vouchers, bundle pricing, landing URLs) before setting up ads
  • Setting up campaign-specific paid ad campaigns on Meta, Google, and TikTok
  • Proposing and obtaining HoM approval for campaign budget uplift
  • Heightened daily monitoring during the live campaign window
  • Contributing paid media results to the post-campaign report

Out of Scope

  • Platform mechanics setup — Shopify, Shopee, Lazada configurations owned by eCommerce Manager
  • Pricing and voucher creation — owned by eCommerce Manager
  • Campaign creative production — owned by Digital Marketing Manager
  • Post-campaign report compilation — owned by Head of Marketing (MM provides paid media data)

Process Flow

  1. Receive campaign brief from Head of Marketing — at least 3 weeks before campaign launch date. Brief must include: campaign name, key dates, target audience, and campaign objective.
  2. Meet with eCommerce Manager to confirm platform mechanics: landing URLs, voucher codes, bundle pricing, and platform-specific promotions. Do not set up ads until landing URLs and mechanics are confirmed.
  3. Propose campaign budget uplift to Head of Marketing (via ClickUp or agreed channel). No campaign-specific budget increase may be executed without explicit HoM approval.
  4. Once budget is approved and creative is received from DMM, set up campaign-specific paid campaigns on applicable platforms with campaign-specific targeting and messaging.
  5. During the live campaign window: monitor daily (morning and afternoon check-in). Reallocate budget in real time to best-performing channels, within approved total.
  6. Within 2 days of campaign end, compile paid media performance data (ROAS, spend, CTR, conversions, CPA) and submit to the Head of Marketing.

Owner: Media Manager

During the campaign live window, check performance in the morning (before 9:30am) and afternoon (around 3pm) every day. Identify best-performing channels and ad sets. Shift budget in real time to maximise ROAS, within the approved total. Flag any critical performance anomalies to the Head of Marketing the same day.

Handoff to: Head of Marketing

Within 2 days of the campaign end date, compile paid media performance data and submit to the Head of Marketing: total spend, ROAS per channel, CTR, conversion rate, CPA, and top-performing creatives.

Purpose

This process ensures all paid media budgets are tracked daily, spending stays within plan, ROAS targets are monitored and flagged, and any budget change — increase or decrease, across any channel — goes through the Head of Marketing for approval before it is executed.

Scope

In Scope

  • Daily spend tracking against the approved media plan
  • ROAS monitoring per channel and campaign
  • Flagging overspend, underspend, and ROAS anomalies to the Head of Marketing
  • Submitting budget change requests to HoM for approval before execution
  • Documenting all approved budget changes with date, amount, rationale, and HoM sign-off

Out of Scope

  • Approving budget changes — this authority rests with the Head of Marketing
  • Setting the initial media budget — set at the quarterly strategy stage (Section 1)
  • Paying media invoices — handled outside the Media Manager's scope
  • Reporting on non-paid channels (email, organic, SEO)

Process Flow

  1. Each business day, pull actual spend per channel from each platform and compare to the daily pacing target (monthly budget ÷ number of days in month).
  2. If actual spend deviates from the pacing target by more than 15%, investigate the cause (algorithm, audience exhaustion, creative issue).
  3. If ROAS falls below the channel target for 3 consecutive days, flag to the Head of Marketing with a brief summary of the cause and proposed action.
  4. If a budget change is needed (up or down), submit a change request to the Head of Marketing with: channel, current budget, proposed budget, reason, and expected impact.
  5. Wait for explicit HoM approval before executing any change. No exceptions.
  6. Once approved, execute the change on the platform and log it in the budget change log: date, channel, change amount, reason, approved by.

Purpose

This process ensures paid media performance is visible, consistent, and actionable across the team. The Media Manager owns the Google Data Studio dashboard, delivers a weekly performance report to the Head of Marketing, and compiles paid media data after each campaign window.

Scope

In Scope

Purpose

This process ensures the Digital Marketing Manager is always informed of how ad creatives are performing across paid channels, so creative fatigue is identified early and refresh briefs are issued before performance drops significantly. The weekly sync is the primary mechanism for this feedback loop between the Media Manager and the Digital Marketing Manager.

Scope

In Scope

  • Weekly 30-minute sync between Media Manager and Digital Marketing Manager
  • MM prepares creative performance data ahead of each sync (CTR, frequency, CPM per creative)
  • Identifying creatives with consistent 2-week CTR decline or frequency above 3 — these trigger a formal creative refresh request
  • Agreeing on a refresh timeline with the DMM during or immediately after the sync
  • Following up on previously agreed refresh requests

Out of Scope

  • Creative production decisions — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Strategic creative direction — owned by the Head of Marketing
  • Organic content planning — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Budget decisions triggered by creative fatigue — require HoM approval (see Section 4)

Process Flow

  1. Before the weekly sync, the Media Manager pulls CTR, frequency, and CPM for every active ad creative across Meta, Google, and TikTok.
  2. Flag any creative with: (a) a consistent CTR decline over 2 consecutive weeks, or (b) ad frequency above 3 per week — these are formal fatigue signals.
  3. In the weekly 30-minute sync with the DMM, share the creative performance data. Walk through flagged creatives and their metrics.
  4. Agree on a refresh timeline for each flagged creative. The DMM commits to a delivery date for the replacement or variation. Document the agreed timeline in ClickUp or the agreed tracking tool.
  5. In the following week's sync, check if agreed refreshes were delivered. If a creative refresh was not delivered by the agreed date and the flagged creative is still running, escalate to the Head of Marketing.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Prepare Creative Performance Data Before the Sync ►
Owner: Media Manager
When: Day before or morning of the weekly sync

Pull CTR, frequency, CPM, and impressions for every active ad creative across Meta, Google, and TikTok. Organise by channel and creative, with week-on-week comparison. This data is the agenda for the sync — do not run the sync without it.

Step 2: Identify and Flag Fatigue Signals ►
Owner: Media Manager

A creative is considered fatigued and triggers a formal refresh request when either of the following conditions is met:

  • CTR decline: CTR has dropped for 2 consecutive weeks with no recovery
  • High frequency: ad frequency has exceeded 3 per week for the target audience

Creatives meeting these criteria are marked as "refresh required" in the performance data going into the sync.

Step 3: Run the Weekly Sync with the DMM ►
With: Digital Marketing Manager
Duration: 30 minutes, weekly

Walk through the creative performance data with the DMM. For each flagged creative, share: the creative name/ID, the channel it runs on, the fatigue signal (CTR trend or frequency), and the current performance vs. the week it launched. The goal is for the DMM to understand the performance context so they can brief a replacement or variation.

Step 4: Agree on Refresh Timeline & Document ►
Owner: Digital Marketing Manager

The DMM commits to a delivery date for each flagged creative refresh. The Media Manager documents the commitment in ClickUp (or the agreed tracking tool): creative name, platform, fatigue signal noted, refresh delivery date agreed, and DMM's name. This creates a record that feeds into the following week's sync check.

Step 5: Follow Up & Escalate if Needed ►
Owner: Media Manager

At the start of each sync, check the status of previously agreed refresh requests. If a creative refresh was not delivered by the agreed date and the fatigued creative is still running on paid channels, escalate to the Head of Marketing. Do not silently continue running a fatigued creative beyond the agreed refresh date.

Exceptions & Edge Cases

SituationAction
DMM is unavailable for the weekly syncReschedule within the same week — do not skip. If this becomes a recurring issue, flag to the Head of Marketing to help protect the sync cadence.
Multiple creatives are fatigued at the same time and the DMM cannot refresh all of them at oncePrioritise by highest spend exposure first. Agree a phased refresh timeline with the DMM and inform the Head of Marketing. Reduce budget on the most fatigued creatives in the interim.
A creative shows CTR decline but frequency is low — could be seasonal or audience saturation rather than creative fatigueNote it as "under observation" rather than issuing a formal refresh request. If the decline continues for a third week, issue the refresh request. Apply context before triggering the process.
