Purpose

This process exists to ensure a consistent, well-managed pipeline of live streamers driving sales for the brand on Shopee and TikTok — from recruitment and onboarding through to performance tracking and payment. It defines how streamers are sourced, filtered, rated, onboarded, equipped, kept engaged, tracked, paid, and supported.

Scope

In Scope

  • Streamer application intake and filtering
  • Rate negotiation and agreement
  • Onboarding via WhatsApp
  • Live stream kit distribution and Shopee/TikTok Seller account access
  • Bi-monthly promotional updates and session follow-up
  • Registering TikTok live sessions for platform voucher eligibility
  • Tracking of sales, commission, media spend, and MER
  • Bi-monthly HR payment reporting
  • Ongoing streamer support and query resolution

Out of Scope

  • KOL Management (Section 2)
  • General eCommerce Support (Section 3)
  • Production/creative direction of the live show format itself
  • Platform-side technical configuration unrelated to streamer access

Process Flow

  1. Streamer applies (form, direct inbound, or referral) and is filtered against criteria, including past brands worked with and sample videos.
  2. Rate is negotiated using the rate card as a baseline; manager sign-off is needed above 10% commission or $25–$50/hour.
  3. Streamer is onboarded via WhatsApp and required details are collected.
  4. Live stream kit and product samples are shared; Shopee and TikTok access is granted.
  5. Promotional updates are sent in Week 1 and Week 3 (two weeks ahead of go-live); under-booked streamers are followed up, and TikTok sessions are registered for voucher eligibility.
  6. Sales, commission, and media spend are logged in the Google Sheet tracker and MER is calculated.
  7. A payment report is sent to HR every Monday evening of Week 2 and Week 4 (latest Tuesday morning).
  8. Streamer questions are answered within 10 minutes during working hours/live sessions; escalations go to the eComm Manager.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Streamer Application Intake & Filtering▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Ongoing, as applications are received
How:
  1. Monitor all three application channels daily: the streamer application form, direct inbound requests (WhatsApp/social DM), and referrals from agencies or existing streamers.
  2. Log every applicant in the streamer pipeline tracker: name, platform(s), follower count/engagement, niche, contact details, and source channel.
  3. Screen each applicant against filtering criteria: platform reach and engagement rate, content/niche fit with the brand, and professionalism or track record (for referrals).
  4. Check which other brands the applicant has previously gone live for, and request sample videos of past live sessions to assess presentation and fit.
  5. Shortlist applicants who meet the criteria and move them to rate negotiation (Step 2). Send a polite decline to applicants who do not qualify.
Output: A shortlist of qualified streamer candidates, logged in the tracker and ready for rate discussion.
Step 2: Rate Negotiation & Agreement▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Once a candidate is shortlisted
How:
  1. Reference the internal rate card to determine the baseline rate for the streamer's tier (e.g. follower/engagement band).
  2. For high-demand or top-tier streamers, negotiate above the card rate where justified.
  3. Manager sign-off is required if the proposed commission exceeds 10% of sales, or if the hourly rate exceeds $25–$50/hour.
  4. Communicate the proposed rate to the streamer, typically via WhatsApp.
  5. Confirm the final agreed rate in writing before proceeding to onboarding, and record it in the streamer tracker.
Output: A documented, agreed rate for the streamer, ready for onboarding.
Step 3: Onboarding via WhatsApp▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Immediately after the rate is agreed
How:
  1. Add the streamer to the onboarding WhatsApp thread.
  2. Send a welcome message covering brand overview, points of contact, and next steps.
  3. Collect required details for setup and payment (e.g. bank/payment information).
  4. Confirm the streamer has the necessary app(s) installed and is ready to proceed to kit distribution and platform access (Step 4).
Output: Streamer onboarded and ready to receive their kit and platform access.
Step 4: Live Stream Kit Distribution & Platform Access▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: After onboarding, before the streamer's first live session
How:
  1. Share the Live Stream Kit via WhatsApp: brand guidelines and talking points, plus arrange delivery or pickup of product samples for the streamer to showcase live.
  2. Grant Shopee Seller account access by collecting the streamer's registered email and mobile number and adding them as an authorized user.
  3. Grant TikTok Shop access by sharing the shared TikTok Seller login credentials securely, reminding the streamer these credentials are confidential and must not be shared further.
  4. Confirm the streamer can log in and access both platforms before their first scheduled session.
Output: Streamer fully equipped with brand materials, product samples, and working platform access.
Step 5: Bi-Monthly Promotional Updates & Session Follow-Up▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Twice per month — Week 1 and Week 3, sent two weeks ahead of each promotion period
How:
  1. Compile upcoming Shopee/TikTok promotions and campaigns relevant to live sessions.
  2. Send the Week 1 update covering promotion mechanics going live in Week 3; send the Week 3 update covering promotion mechanics going live in the following month's Week 1.
  3. Broadcast the update to all active streamers via WhatsApp, highlighting promotions and available session slots to register for.
  4. Cross-check the session tracker for streamers who have registered for fewer than 4 live sessions in the month.
  5. Send a personalized follow-up message to those streamers, encouraging them to register for their preferred session slots.
  6. For sessions on TikTok, additionally register each live stream via TikTok's LIVE registration so it is recognised by TikTok and eligible for additional platform vouchers.
Output: Streamers kept informed of promotions; under-booked streamers re-engaged and prompted to register; TikTok sessions registered for voucher eligibility.
Step 6: Performance Tracking — Sales, Commission, Media Spend & MER▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Ongoing; updated after each live session, at minimum weekly
How:
  1. Open the existing Google Sheet tracker used for this purpose.
  2. Record, per streamer per session: date, platform, recorded sales, commission earned, and media budget spent behind the session.
  3. Ensure the Marketing Efficiency Ratio (MER = Total Sales Revenue ÷ Total Media Spend) is calculated correctly per streamer/session and rolled up to a brand-level MER.
  4. Flag any discrepancies (missing sales data, unclear media spend) to Marketing or Finance for resolution before they feed into reporting.
Output: An accurate, up-to-date record of sales, commission, and MER for the brand.
Step 7: HR Payment Reporting (Bi-Monthly)▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Twice per month — Week 2 and Week 4, sent every Monday evening (at the latest, Tuesday morning)
How:
  1. Pull the latest figures from the Google Sheet tracker for the relevant reporting period.
  2. Compile the HR payment report, including: streamer name and bank/payment details, sessions completed and agreed rate, and sales and commission earned.
  3. Review figures for accuracy against the tracker before submission.
  4. Send the report to HR every Monday evening of Week 2 and Week 4, at the latest by Tuesday morning, so payment can be processed on schedule.
Output: HR receives accurate, timely data to process streamer payments twice a month.
Step 8: Streamer Support & Query Resolution▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Ongoing, as needed
How:
  1. Monitor WhatsApp for questions from live streamers regarding schedules, rates, platform access, or session logistics.
  2. Respond to queries within 10 minutes during working hours and during live sessions.
  3. Resolve straightforward issues directly; escalate rate disputes, contractual issues, or anything beyond the Executive's authority to the eCommerce Manager.
Output: Streamers feel supported; issues are resolved promptly or escalated appropriately.

Purpose

This process exists to identify, vet, and secure relevant social media creators (KOLs) across the brand portfolio — Shortcutx, Tradishot, Solulu Beauty, and Omiee — on TikTok and Instagram. It covers discovery, direct outreach, content and rate negotiation, and handoff to each brand's manager for execution.

Scope

In Scope

  • Creator discovery and identification on TikTok and Instagram, per brand-specific criteria
  • Duplicate/history checks before outreach
  • Direct outreach via each brand's own social media accounts
  • Content format briefing and rate negotiation
  • Logging confirmed creators in ClickUp and assigning to the brand manager for review

Out of Scope

  • Creative revisions once a creator is onboarded
  • Ongoing communication with the creator after ClickUp assignment
  • Invoicing and billing
  • Content shooting, filming, or editing
  • Managing the ClickUp card after it has been assigned to the brand manager
Note: unlike Live Streaming (Section 1), no manager check-in is required before reaching out to a creator — the eCommerce Executive proceeds with outreach directly once a candidate is identified.

Process Flow

  1. Search TikTok & Instagram to identify creators matching brand-specific criteria and monthly volume targets, aiming for ~4x the volume of creators actually needed.
  2. Check the creator's DM history on Instagram/TikTok to confirm there's no duplicate or ongoing outreach.
  3. Reach out directly using the brand's official social media account and outreach template, customized to the creator's content where possible.
  4. If there's no response after 3 days, send one follow-up message; if still no response, move on.
  5. Once the creator responds with interest, brief them on the content format and ask for their rate for 1 IG Reel (Collab) + 1 month of loading.
  6. If the quote exceeds the remaining budget, negotiate a lower rate, emphasizing the simple/authentic format required.
  7. Create a new ClickUp card with the creator's details, rate, loading fee, and period, and move it through the pipeline stages.
  8. Assign the card to the brand's manager for review once terms are confirmed — the Executive's involvement ends there.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Creator Discovery & Identification▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Ongoing, to meet each brand's monthly onboarding target
How:
  1. Search TikTok and Instagram for creators whose look, demeanour, and content match the brand's persona and interest criteria (see Brand Creator Criteria table).
  2. Consider a mix of macro and micro creators — there is no strict follower threshold; the priority is meeting each brand's monthly volume target with relevant, on-brand creators.
  3. Aim to reach out to at least 4x the number of creators needed, to account for response and conversion rates — e.g. Shortcutx should contact roughly 40 candidates/month to onboard 10; Solulu, Tradishot, and Omiee roughly 12-20/month to onboard 3-5.
  4. Track progress against each brand's monthly onboarding target: Shortcutx (10), Solulu (3-5), Tradishot (3-5), Omiee (3-5).
  5. Note the creator's profile link and a few specific details from their feed to personalize the outreach message later.
Output: A shortlist of on-brand creator candidates per brand, ready for a duplicate check.
Step 2: Duplicate / History Check▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Before reaching out to each candidate
How:
  1. Check the creator's DM history on the brand's Instagram or TikTok account for any prior communication or engagement.
  2. If there's a recent or ongoing conversation, continue in the existing thread rather than sending a fresh outreach message.
  3. If a prior conversation was already declined or closed out, use judgement on whether to re-approach or move to the next candidate.
Output: Confirmation that outreach won't duplicate or contradict a prior conversation.
Step 3: Direct Outreach via Brand Social Accounts▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: After the duplicate check — no manager check-in required before proceeding
How:
  1. Log in to the relevant brand's Instagram/TikTok account (login details provided separately).
  2. Send the brand-specific outreach message (see Outreach Message Templates below), customizing it with a reference to the creator's content or feed where possible.
  3. If there is no response after 3 days, send one follow-up message.
  4. If there is still no response after the follow-up, move on to the next candidate — no further follow-up is sent.
Output: Creator has been contacted; their response (or lack of one) determines the next step.
Step 4: Content Format Briefing & Rate Request▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Once a creator responds with interest
How:
  1. Explain the content requirement: a simple talking-head video, no heavy editing, subtitles and key points overlaid, 30 seconds to 1 minute long.
  2. Ask the creator for their rate for 1 IG Reel (Collab) + 1 month of platform loading fee.
  3. For Omiee specifically, clarify upfront that this is a barter collaboration — product is sent in exchange for one video, with no cash payment discussed.
Output: A rate quote from the creator (or a confirmed barter agreement for Omiee).
Step 5: Rate Review & Negotiation▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Once the creator's rate quote is received
How:
  1. Compare the quoted rate (content creation + loading fee) against the brand's remaining monthly KOL budget: Shortcutx $9,000/month, Solulu $1,000/month, Tradishot $3,000/month — all inclusive of content creation, ads, and platform loading fees. Omiee has no cash budget; it is barter-only.
  2. If the quote exceeds what's available, negotiate directly with the creator for a lower rate, emphasizing that the brand wants a simple, authentic talking-head video rather than a highly stylised or heavily produced one.
  3. Confirm the final agreed rate (or barter terms for Omiee) and the collaboration period with the creator.
Output: A finalized rate and collaboration period, ready to be logged in ClickUp.
Step 6: Log Creator in ClickUp & Assign for Review▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Once terms are confirmed with the creator
How:
  1. Open the relevant brand's ClickUp list (see Related Documents for links: Shortcutx, Tradishot, Solulu, Omiee).
  2. Create a new card for the creator with: name, profile link, content creation rate, loading fee, and collaboration period.
  3. Move the card through the pipeline stages as the conversation progresses (e.g. New → Outreach Sent → Rate Confirmed, plus any additional stages already set up in each brand's ClickUp list).
  4. Once terms are confirmed, assign the card to the relevant brand's manager for their review.
Output: A complete, assigned ClickUp card, ready for the brand manager to take over.

Purpose

This process exists to give the business accurate, consistent, weekly visibility into sales performance across all eCommerce channels — Shopify, Shopee, TikTok, and Lazada — for every brand, and to keep stakeholders informed of upcoming platform campaigns. It supports clean month-to-month trend comparison via the shared Canva sales performance reporting file.

Reporting File Structure

The Canva file contains one slide per brand (Shortcutx, Tradishot, Solulu Beauty, Omiee), plus a cover slide stating the current reporting period (e.g. "6/7–12/7"). Each brand's slide includes:

  • The brand logo and an "eCommerce Sales Report" title
  • A "Campaign Calendar" listing upcoming campaigns per platform, with dates
  • A stacked bar chart with one bar per platform the brand is active on (not every brand sells on all four platforms) — each week adds a new stacked segment in a new colour, labelled with its date range
  • A footer note showing the previous month's final total per platform, for month-to-month comparison

Scope

In Scope

  • Weekly (every Monday, from 10am) data pull from Shopify, Shopee, TikTok, and Lazada, per brand
  • Updating the Canva sales performance reporting file with revenue/GMV figures
  • Starting a new chart each calendar month and closing it on the last day of that month
  • Referencing the upcoming campaigns Canva file and reflecting relevant campaigns per platform in the report
  • Notifying the brand's eCommerce Manager and Head of Marketing via Slack/Teams once updated

Out of Scope

  • Building or redesigning the Canva file's chart templates (only updating data within existing charts)
  • Campaign planning or creation (only referencing the campaigns file maintained elsewhere)
Note: monthly eCommerce reporting support has been confirmed as an additional upcoming responsibility for this section — its exact scope and steps are still to be defined and will be added once briefed.

Process Flow

  1. Every Monday at 10am, log into Shopify, Shopee, TikTok, and Lazada for each brand.
  2. Pull last week's (Monday–Sunday) revenue/GMV from each platform's reporting section.
  3. Check whether the current month already has an active chart in the Canva file; start a new chart on the 1st if not.
  4. Add the week's revenue/GMV data point(s) to the correct month's chart, per brand and platform.
  5. Reference the upcoming campaigns Canva file and update the campaigns section per platform.
  6. If any platform's data looks off or is inaccessible, flag it with a note and proceed with available data.
  7. Message the brand's eCommerce Manager and Head of Marketing on Slack/Teams once the update is complete.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Weekly Data Extraction▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Every Monday, from 10am (data may not be ready before this time)
How:
  1. Log into each brand's Shopify admin, Shopee Seller Center, TikTok Shop Seller Center, and Lazada Seller Center (login details provided separately).
  2. Pull this week's revenue/GMV per brand, per platform: Shopify → Analytics → filter by time period; Shopee → Data → Business Insights → filter by date (adding each date individually for the period); TikTok → Data → Business Insights → filter by time period; Lazada → Data Insight → Business Advisor.
  3. Only pull data for platforms the brand actually sells on — not every brand is active on all four platforms (e.g. Solulu has no Shopify presence; Omiee currently sells only via Shopee and TikTok).
  4. Record net revenue/GMV — i.e. after returns and cancellations — not gross, for every platform.
  5. Record the figures for each brand/platform combination before moving to the Canva file.
Output: A complete set of this week's revenue/GMV figures for every brand, across the platforms each brand is actually active on.
Step 2: Canva Chart Update▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Immediately after data extraction, same Monday
How:
  1. Open the sales performance reporting file in Canva. Update the cover slide's period label to the current reporting window (e.g. "6/7–12/7").
  2. Confirm whether a chart already exists for the current calendar month on each brand's slide; if not, start a new chart beginning on the 1st of the month — never on the 30th, 2nd, or any other mid-month date. The first weekly segment of a new month runs from the 1st through the end of that first partial week.
  3. On each brand's slide, add this week's revenue/GMV as a new stacked segment (in a new legend colour) on top of the existing bar, per platform the brand is active on — each bar is one platform (Shopify, Shopee, TikTok, Lazada), stacked week by week through the month. Label the new segment with its exact date range (e.g. "6/7–12/7") in the legend.
  4. Ensure the final segment of the month ends on the last calendar day of that month, even if it's a partial week; never let it stretch into the next month, so month-to-month comparison stays clean.
  5. When starting a new month's chart, update the footer note on each brand's slide with the previous month's final total per platform (e.g. "June 2026: Shopify $179,142 | Shopee $436,751 | TikTok $23,338 | Lazada $15,095") for month-to-month comparison.
Output: An up-to-date monthly chart per brand, with this week's data stacked on, ready for the next comparison point.
Step 3: Upcoming Campaigns Update▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Same Monday, after the chart is updated
How:
  1. Open the upcoming campaigns Canva file.
  2. Review campaigns planned per platform for the coming period, for each brand.
  3. Update the "Campaign Calendar" section on each brand's slide in the sales performance reporting file, listing the campaign name and date range per platform.
  4. Leave out platforms the brand isn't running campaigns on for that brand, consistent with the file's existing convention (e.g. marked "NA" or simply omitted).
Output: Each brand's slide reflects both past performance and upcoming campaign context, matching the platforms that brand is actually active on.
Step 4: Data Quality Check▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Throughout data extraction and chart update
How:
  1. Cross-check pulled figures against each platform's dashboard totals for the period.
  2. If a discrepancy is found, or a platform's Business Insights page is inaccessible, proceed with the data that is available rather than delaying the report.
  3. Add a note in the reporting file flagging the specific gap or discrepancy.
  4. Follow up to resolve and backfill the figure once the issue is fixed.
Output: The report is published on time even when data hiccups occur, with issues transparently flagged rather than silently omitted.
Step 5: Stakeholder Notification▶
Who: eCommerce Executive
When: Once the chart and campaigns section are both updated
How:
  1. Send a Slack/Teams message to the brand's eCommerce Manager and the Head of Marketing.
  2. Include a link to the updated Canva file and a one-line summary of the week (e.g. notable movement, flagged data gaps).
Output: Stakeholders are promptly informed that the week's report is ready to review.

Exceptions and Edge Cases

ScenarioWhat to Do
Data not yet available by 10am Monday (platform lag)Proceed with the platforms that are ready, flag the missing ones, update the chart once available, then notify stakeholders.
New brand or platform added mid-monthStart that brand/platform's chart from its first full week, and note the mid-month start in the file.
A public holiday falls within the reporting weekStill record the actual data for the week; add a note if the holiday visibly affected sales.
Platform seller center login fails or access is revokedEscalate to whoever manages the platform credentials to restore access before proceeding.
Campaigns file hasn't been updated by Marketing that weekUse the last known campaign info, note that it may be outdated, and prompt Marketing for the latest version.
A brand isn't active on a given platform (e.g. Solulu has no Shopify presence)Omit that platform's bar and campaign entry entirely for that brand — don't show a zero or blank placeholder.
