Purpose

This process exists to ensure eCommerce sales campaigns across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada are pre-planned one quarter ahead — covering all 3 months of the upcoming quarter, so GWP (gift-with-purchase) sourcing has sufficient runway — and are registered, given accurate discount/bundle mechanics, and set up on each platform in close collaboration with the eCommerce Executive, with Head of Marketing approval on the calendar and mechanics before anything goes live.

Scope

In Scope

  • Planning the eCommerce campaign calendar one quarter ahead — covering all 3 months of the upcoming quarter — across Shopify (own site), TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada, to ensure sufficient runway for GWP (gift-with-purchase) sourcing
  • Selecting which platform-run campaigns to participate in (6.6, 9.9, 11.11, 12.12, and platform-specific events) and submitting registration directly via each platform's seller center
  • Setting campaign mechanics: discount percentages, bundle offers, and voucher structures
  • Submitting the campaign calendar and mechanics to the Head of Marketing for approval
  • Briefing the Digital Marketing Manager (creative) and the in-house Media Manager (paid media) on upcoming campaigns, including a call with the Media Manager to align on required assets
  • Setting up and launching campaigns on each platform, working hand-in-hand with the eCommerce Executive — the eCommerce Manager holds ultimate accountability for accurate, on-time setup

Out of Scope

  • Producing campaign creative (ad video/static, on-platform banners and assets) — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager (Process Documentation, Section 4: eCommerce Campaigns)
  • The eCommerce Executive's own day-to-day platform execution outside campaigns (e.g. listing/catalog maintenance) — covered under the eCommerce Executive's own process documentation
  • Live streaming and KOL management — covered under the eCommerce Executive's own process documentation

Process Flow

  1. Plan the eCommerce campaign calendar one quarter ahead — covering all 3 months of the upcoming quarter — across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada, factoring in platform-run campaign windows and flagging GWP sourcing needs early.
  2. Decide which platform-run campaigns to participate in, and submit registration directly via each platform's seller center.
  3. Set campaign mechanics — discount percentages, bundles, and vouchers — for each campaign.
  4. Share the draft calendar and mechanics with the Digital Marketing Manager, so campaign creative production can begin against the required lead time, and brief the Media Manager on upcoming campaigns via a call to align on required paid media assets.
  5. Submit the calendar and mechanics to the Head of Marketing for approval.
  6. If not approved, revise and resubmit.
  7. Once approved, and once creative is ready, set up and launch the campaign on each platform, working hand-in-hand with the eCommerce Executive.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Plan Campaign Calendar▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: One quarter ahead of the upcoming quarter — all 3 months of the upcoming quarter's calendar must be planned during the current quarter, to give GWP (gift-with-purchase) sourcing sufficient runway
How:
  1. Map out the campaign calendar across Shopify (own site), TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada, covering all 3 months of the upcoming quarter.
  2. Factor in major platform-run campaign windows (6.6, 9.9, 11.11, 12.12, and platform-specific events) alongside internally-driven promos.
  3. Flag any campaigns requiring GWP (gift-with-purchase) early, so sourcing, design, production, and delivery have sufficient lead time.
Output: A draft campaign calendar covering all 3 months of the upcoming quarter across all platforms, with GWP requirements flagged early.
Step 2: Register for Platform Campaigns▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Per each platform's registration deadline
How:
  1. Decide which platform-run campaigns to participate in.
  2. Submit registration directly via each platform's seller center (TikTok Shop, Shopee, Lazada).
Output: Confirmed campaign participation across platforms.
Step 3: Set Campaign Mechanics▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Following campaign calendar planning
How:
  1. Define discount percentages, bundle offers, and voucher structures for each campaign.
  2. Ensure mechanics align with margin and inventory considerations.
Output: Defined campaign mechanics, ready for approval and creative briefing.
Step 4: Brief Digital Marketing Manager & Media Manager▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: As soon as the calendar and mechanics are drafted, respecting the Digital Marketing Manager's required lead time (4-6 weeks for major campaigns) and giving the Media Manager enough runway to plan paid media
How:
  1. Share the campaign calendar, dates, and mechanics with the Digital Marketing Manager, so campaign creative can be produced to the required lead time.
  2. Brief the Media Manager (in-house) on upcoming campaigns and set up a call to discuss required paid media assets and boosting/dark-ad needs.
  3. Confirm both the Digital Marketing Manager and Media Manager have what's needed to proceed.
Output: The Digital Marketing Manager and Media Manager briefed and able to begin creative and paid media planning.
Step 5: Submit for Approval▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Once the calendar and mechanics are finalized; approval targeted within 1 week of submission
How:
  1. Present the campaign calendar and mechanics to the Head of Marketing for approval, consulting with the Media Manager, Warehouse & Production (for stock issues), and CEO.
  2. Target turnaround is 1 week from submission to approval.
  3. Revise and resubmit if not approved.
Output: An approved campaign calendar and mechanics, cleared within 1 week.
Step 6: Set Up & Launch on Platforms▶
Who: eCommerce Manager, working hand-in-hand with the eCommerce Executive
When: Once mechanics are approved and creative is ready
How:
  1. Configure the campaign on each platform (Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, Lazada) — pricing, discounts, bundles, and campaign pages/banners.
  2. Instruct and request help from the eCommerce Executive as needed, providing a demonstration of what's required, including a call to walk through it.
  3. All pricing setting, setup, and final output remain the eCommerce Manager's responsibility.
  4. Verify the campaign is live and configured correctly on each platform before the campaign start time.
Output: Campaigns live, accurately configured, and on time across all platforms.

Purpose

This process exists to continuously monitor storefront and listing performance across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada — listing health, traffic/conversion signals, competitor and price positioning, and ad/placement performance and budget, including daily confirmation that both GMV Products and GMV Live ads have sufficient budget to keep running — so that content, pricing, placement, and budget can be optimized on an ongoing basis.

Scope

In Scope

  • Monitoring listing health across all platforms (out-of-stock, broken, delisted, or flagged listings)
  • Monitoring traffic and conversion signals (CTR, conversion rate, add-to-cart drop-off) to identify underperforming listings
  • Monitoring competitor pricing and promos on the same platforms
  • Monitoring platform ad/sponsored-placement performance and ad budget pacing, including a daily check that sufficient budget is available to keep both GMV Products and GMV Live ads running
  • Revising listing content (titles, images, descriptions) and pricing/placement/bidding on an ongoing basis, based on performance data

Out of Scope

  • Campaign calendar planning, registration, and mechanics — covered under Section 1: Campaign Calendar & Platform Ops
  • Price accuracy correction and promo/live-slot registrations — covered under Section 3: Price Accuracy & Promo/Live Registrations
  • Producing new creative assets — owned by the Digital Marketing Manager
  • Paid social ad strategy and execution — owned by the Media Manager

Process Flow

  1. Monitor listing health, traffic/conversion signals, competitor/price positioning, and ad/placement performance and budget across all platforms.
  2. Diagnose issues and opportunities from the combined signals.
  3. Decide whether content, pricing, placement, or budget adjustments are warranted.
  4. If yes, revise listing content and/or adjust pricing, placement, or bidding.
  5. If no, continue monitoring.
  6. Track the impact of any revisions made, and feed learnings back into the next monitoring cycle.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Monitor Listing Health▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Weekly, across all platforms
How:
  1. Check for out-of-stock, broken, delisted, or flagged listings across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada.
  2. Flag any listing content or image errors found.
  3. Resolve text- or pricing-based issues within 4 hours of detection; resolve issues requiring image edits within 1 business day.
Output: A current view of listing health issues across all platforms, resolved within target turnaround.
Step 2: Monitor Traffic & Conversion Signals▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Weekly, across all platforms
How:
  1. Track click-through rate, conversion rate, and add-to-cart drop-off by listing.
  2. Flag underperforming listings relative to their historical baseline or category peers.
Output: A list of underperforming listings needing attention.
Step 3: Monitor Competitor & Price Positioning▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Weekly, across all platforms
How:
  1. Check competitor pricing and promos on the same platforms.
  2. Note any moves that materially affect Shortcutx's competitiveness.
Output: An up-to-date view of competitive positioning.
Step 4: Monitor Ad/Placement Performance & Budget▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Weekly for ad/placement performance review; daily for GMV Products & GMV Live budget sufficiency checks
How:
  1. Track platform ad and sponsored-placement performance (TikTok Shop ads, Shopee/Lazada sponsored listings), reviewed weekly.
  2. Check daily that sufficient budget is available to keep both GMV Products and GMV Live ads running without interruption.
  3. Track ad budget spend and pacing against allocation.
  4. If budget is insufficient, understand why the shortfall occurred and resolve it with Finance; flag to the Head of Marketing immediately.
Output: A current view of ad/placement performance and budget pacing, with GMV Products and GMV Live ads confirmed to have sufficient running budget.
Step 5: Diagnose & Decide on Action▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Following each monitoring cycle
How:
  1. Review findings from listing health, traffic/conversion, competitor, and ad/budget monitoring together.
  2. Decide whether content, pricing, placement, or budget adjustments are warranted.
Output: A clear decision on what, if anything, needs to be revised.
Step 6: Revise & Track Impact▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Ongoing, as issues/opportunities are identified
How:
  1. Revise listing content (titles, images, descriptions) where content is the driver.
  2. Adjust pricing, placement, or bidding where that is the driver.
  3. Track the impact of each revision against pre-change performance.
Output: Listings and campaigns continuously optimized based on performance data.

Exceptions & Edge Cases

Traffic or conversion drops more than 20% week-over-week for a listing (proposed threshold)Check for listing errors, stockouts, or a competitor move before assuming a demand issue; escalate to the Head of Marketing if unresolved within 48 hours.
Ad budget is pacing more than 20% over or under the planned monthly budget by the mid-month point (proposed threshold)Flag to the Media Manager and adjust pacing; escalate to the Head of Marketing if a budget reallocation is needed.
Insufficient budget to keep GMV Products or GMV Live ads runningUnderstand why the shortfall occurred, resolve with Finance, and flag to the Head of Marketing immediately.

Purpose

This process exists to ensure pricing stays accurate across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada at all times, and to capture platform promo and live-selling opportunities outside major campaigns — registering for available slots and coordinating execution with the eCommerce Executive.

Scope

In Scope

  • Monitoring price accuracy across all platforms against approved pricing and campaign mechanics
  • Correcting price discrepancies immediately when found
  • Tracking and registering for platform promo slots (flash sales, voucher programs) outside major campaigns
  • Tracking and registering for live-selling slots (TikTok LIVE, Shopee Live) outside major campaigns
  • Coordinating live-selling execution with the eCommerce Executive

Out of Scope

  • Major campaign registration and mechanics — covered under Section 1: Campaign Calendar & Platform Ops
  • Live streaming execution itself — owned by the eCommerce Executive (Process Documentation, Live Streaming Management)
  • Broader listing/traffic/ad monitoring — covered under Section 2: Platform Monitoring & Optimization

Process Flow

  1. Monitor live pricing across all platforms against approved pricing and mechanics.
  2. If a discrepancy is found, correct it immediately and verify.
  3. Track upcoming promo slots and live-selling slots outside major campaigns, and assess fit against the calendar and stock.
  4. If worth pursuing, register/apply for the slot via each platform's seller center.
  5. Coordinate execution with the eCommerce Executive, who owns live-selling execution.
  6. Update the internal calendar with confirmed slots and pricing status.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Monitor Price Accuracy▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Weekly, across all platforms
How:
  1. Check live pricing on Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada against the master price list and campaign mechanics.
  2. Identify any discrepancies between the master price list and live pricing.
  3. Before flagging as a discrepancy, check that the difference isn't explained by a voucher or discount made available by the platform itself.
Output: Confirmed pricing accuracy, or a list of genuine discrepancies to correct.
Step 2: Correct Pricing Discrepancies▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Immediately upon discovery
How:
  1. Correct the price on the affected platform(s) as soon as a discrepancy is found.
  2. Verify the correction is live and accurate.
Output: Accurate, corrected pricing live on all platforms.
Step 3: Track Promo & Live-Selling Slot Windows▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Ongoing
How:
  1. Track upcoming platform promo slots (flash sales, voucher programs) and live-selling slots (TikTok LIVE, Shopee Live) outside major campaigns.
  2. Assess fit against the campaign calendar and stock availability.
Output: A shortlist of promo/live slots worth pursuing.
Step 4: Register for Promo & Live-Selling Slots▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: Within 48 hours of a platform opening registration for a slot, and no later than 24 hours before the registration window closes
How:
  1. Register/apply for selected promo or live-selling slots directly via each platform's seller center, targeting within 48 hours of the registration window opening.
  2. If the 48-hour target is missed, ensure registration is still submitted no later than 24 hours before the window closes.
  3. Confirm slot approval and note the details (date, time, mechanics/requirements).
Output: Confirmed promo/live-selling slot registrations, submitted within the target window.
Step 5: Coordinate Execution with eCommerce Executive▶
Who: eCommerce Manager, coordinating with the eCommerce Executive
When: The eCommerce Executive books streamers into the slot once it's confirmed; key streamer sessions are blocked on the calendar immediately upon confirmed availability
How:
  1. Hand off live-selling slot details to the eCommerce Executive, who books streamers into the slot and owns live streaming execution.
  2. For key streamers specifically, block the session on the calendar immediately upon confirmation — confirmation is only given after checking the streaming calendar for availability.
  3. Confirm promo slot mechanics are reflected correctly on the platform ahead of the live window.
Output: Promo and live-selling slots executed as planned, with key streamer sessions locked in immediately upon confirmed availability.
Step 6: Update Internal Calendar▶
Who: eCommerce Manager
When: After each registration or correction
How:
  1. Log confirmed promo/live-selling slots and any price corrections made.
  2. Keep the internal calendar current for visibility across the team.
Output: An internal calendar that reflects confirmed slots and pricing status.

Exceptions & Edge Cases

ScenarioWhat to Do
A price discrepancy is found (live price differs from the master price list, and isn't explained by a platform voucher/discount)Correct immediately and escalate to the Head of Marketing; assess if any orders were affected.
A promo or live-selling slot conflicts with the campaign calendarPrioritize based on expected impact; consult the Head of Marketing if unclear.
A live-selling slot is confirmed but the eCommerce Executive is unavailableEscalate to the Head of Marketing to find coverage or release the slot in time.
A platform changes promo/live slot terms after registrationReassess fit and flag to the Head of Marketing if the change affects mechanics or stock commitments.

Purpose

This process exists to keep sales, traffic, and conversion performance across Shopify, TikTok Shop, Shopee, and Lazada visible on a regular cadence — a weekly summary to the Head of Marketing, and a fuller monthly report presented no later than the 10th of the month to the Head of Marketing, CEO, Brand Manager, and each brand's co-founder — so performance trends and issues are surfaced and acted on in time.

Scope

In Scope

  • Tracking daily sales, traffic, and conversion performance across all platforms
  • Compiling and submitting a weekly performance summary to the Head of Marketing
  • Attending the weekly paid media performance sync with the Media Manager and Head of Marketing; agreeing on creative refresh timelines and briefing the Digital Marketing Manager on refresh needs within 24 hours of the sync
  • Compiling and presenting a fuller monthly performance report, no later than the 10th of the month, to the Head of Marketing, CEO, Brand Manager, and each brand's co-founder (or shared as a soft copy if attendance isn't possible)
  • Capturing follow-up actions from reporting discussions and routing them into calendar planning or optimization work

Out of Scope

  • Acting on the findings (calendar changes, listing revisions, price corrections) — covered under Sections 1-3
  • Social media and KOL reporting — covered under the Digital Marketing Manager's process documentation

Process Flow

  1. Track daily sales, traffic, and conversion performance across all platforms.
  2. Compile a weekly performance summary and submit it to the Head of Marketing.
  3. Attend the weekly paid media performance sync with the Media Manager and Head of Marketing. Review paid channel performance data (CTR, ROAS, frequency), align on optimisation priorities, and agree on creative refresh timelines for any fatigued creatives.
  4. Within 24 hours of the sync, brief the Digital Marketing Manager on which creatives need refreshing, the fatigue signal, and the agreed delivery timeline. Log the refresh tasks in ClickUp.
  5. Compile a fuller monthly performance report covering trends, campaigns, and platform breakdown.
  6. Present the monthly report to the Head of Marketing.
  7. Capture follow-up actions and route them into campaign calendar planning, platform monitoring/optimization, or price/promo work.
