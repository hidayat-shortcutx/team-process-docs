Purpose

This process exists to keep the Shopify storefront's homepage and banners refreshed and aligned with current campaigns and promotions, with proper design clearance from the Head of Marketing and quality assurance before and after each update goes live.

Scope

In Scope

  • Homepage banner design and creation, for both planned campaigns and ad-hoc always-on promos
  • Requesting and tracking approval from the Head of Marketing via ClickUp before publishing
  • Publishing approved banner updates live on Shopify
  • Post-publish QA of the live banner (rendering, links, load time)

Out of Scope

  • Landing page builds
  • Product and collection page updates
  • Site health, speed, and performance monitoring — owned outside this role (e.g. developer/agency), not part of the Digital Marketing Manager's responsibilities
  • App or plugin management

Process Flow

  1. Reference the campaign calendar or an ad-hoc promo request to determine the homepage banner update needed.
  2. Design the banner asset (desktop and mobile versions) to match the campaign or promo.
  3. Create a ClickUp card with the draft asset and submit it to the Head of Marketing for review.
  4. If not approved at least 3 days before the live date, follow up and escalate — never publish without clearance.
  5. Once approved, publish the banner update live on the Shopify storefront.
  6. Run the QA checklist (desktop/mobile rendering, links, images, load time) to confirm everything displays correctly.
  7. Repeat at least twice a month based on the campaign calendar, plus any ad-hoc always-on promos as they arise.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Identify Update Requirement▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Ongoing — driven by the campaign calendar (minimum twice per month) and ad-hoc requests
How:
  1. Reference the marketing/campaign calendar to identify upcoming promotions that require a homepage banner update.
  2. Monitor for ad-hoc requests from brand or eCommerce managers for always-on promos outside the planned calendar.
  3. Confirm the messaging, dates, and offer details for the banner with the requesting stakeholder where needed.
Output: A clear brief of what the banner needs to communicate and by when.
Step 2: Design the Banner Asset▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: After the update requirement is identified
How:
  1. Design the homepage banner/promotional asset, including both desktop and mobile versions, reflecting the campaign or promo messaging.
  2. Ensure the asset matches brand guidelines and Shopify's homepage banner specifications.
Output: A draft banner asset ready for review.
Step 3: Submit for Approval via ClickUp▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Once the draft asset is ready
How:
  1. Create a ClickUp card with the draft banner asset attached, tagging the Head of Marketing for review.
  2. Include the intended live date and relevant campaign details in the card.
  3. If the Head of Marketing hasn't approved by 3 days before the intended live date, send a follow-up/escalation — do not publish without clearance.
Output: An approved banner asset, tracked in ClickUp, ready to publish.
Step 4: Publish to Shopify▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Once approval is received in ClickUp
How:
  1. Upload and publish the approved banner asset live on the Shopify homepage.
  2. Confirm the correct desktop and mobile versions are set and scheduled for the right dates.
Output: The updated banner is live on the storefront.
Step 5: Post-Publish QA▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Immediately after publishing
How:
  1. Check the banner renders correctly on both desktop and mobile, matching the approved design (no layout shift, cropping, or distortion).
  2. Test that any linked buttons/CTAs go to the correct destination.
  3. Confirm the copy matches the approved draft exactly (dates, pricing, promo codes, spelling).
  4. Confirm the previous banner is fully replaced, with no overlap or flash of old content.
  5. Confirm the page loads without noticeable slowdown and all images display (no broken image icons).
  6. Spot-check on at least two browsers (e.g. Chrome and Safari) for consistent rendering.
Output: Confirmation that the live banner displays and functions correctly.

Exceptions and Edge Cases

ScenarioWhat to Do

An urgent, always-on promo needs a fast turnaroundStill route through ClickUp for approval, but flag the card as urgent for expedited review.
QA check finds an issue after the banner is already liveFix the issue immediately, or revert to the previous banner while the fix is made.
Campaign dates change after the banner is already approved/liveUpdate the banner and re-submit for a quick approval check before republishing.

Purpose

This process exists to ensure consistent, effective email marketing through Klaviyo — planning and executing monthly campaign EDMs across brands, and keeping automated flows (welcome, abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back) accurate and performing well, with Head of Marketing approval before any campaign send.

Scope

In Scope

  • Planning the monthly campaign/promo EDM lineup per brand, tied to the campaign calendar
  • Defining segmentation rules for each planned EDM
  • Drafting individual EDM content in Klaviyo
  • Submitting the monthly lineup, segmentation rules, and drafts to the Head of Marketing for approval via Google Docs
  • Scheduling and sending approved campaign EDMs
  • Maintaining automated flows: adding new products, removing discontinued products, refreshing stale content
  • Identifying gaps in the customer journey and briefing in missing flows (proposed copy + trigger logic)
  • Ongoing monitoring of open rate, click rate, revenue attribution, and flow conversion, with proposed fixes for notable changes
  • Quarterly review of automated flow performance, with suggested edits

Out of Scope

  • Graphic/creative asset design beyond what's built directly in Klaviyo (if applicable)
  • SMS marketing — not currently run for any brand
  • List/subscriber acquisition tactics (e.g. website signup forms) — covered under other sections
Note: Campaigns are the monthly, calendar-driven EDM sends covered by Steps 1-5. Flows are always-on and triggered by customer activity, covered by Steps 6-9.

Reference: EDM Tier System

EDMs are run on a 4-tier system, defined in the Shortcutx EDM Playbook 2026 and adopted brand-wide (Shortcutx, Tradishot, Solulu, Omiee), which governs tone, discounting, and CTA style for every campaign email. The full playbook also covers copy standards, brand voice rules, the standard trust footer, and the ClickUp brief format — see Related Documents for the link.

TierPurposeTarget/MonthDiscountCTA Style
A — Relationship & TrustBuild brand affinity, humanise the brand (founder notes, testimonials, FAQs)2-3 emailsNone (store credit at most)Soft — "Read more," "Shop our range"
B — Education & Product DepthInform, differentiate, build product confidence (ingredient spotlights, routines)1-2 emailsNoneMedium — "Shop [Product]," "Learn about [ingredient]"
C — Collection & Social ProofShowcase range, build desire, drive browsing (collections, bestsellers)1-2 emailsNoneMedium — "Explore the collection"
D — PromotionalDrive direct purchases with urgency (campaign launches, flash sales)2-3 emailsAlways present (anchor pricing)Strong — "Shop Now," "Get X% Off"

Frequency rules: minimum 8 emails/month, maximum 2/week outside promo periods, minimum 2-3 days between sends, and no more than 2 consecutive Tier D emails without an A or B tier in between.

Process Flow

  1. Reference the campaign calendar to plan the month's EDM lineup, per brand (Solulu and Omiee once each brand's website launches, targeting 6 EDMs/month).
  2. Define segmentation rules for each planned EDM.
  3. Draft each EDM's content in Klaviyo.
  4. Compile the lineup, segmentation rules, and drafts into a Google Doc and submit to the Head of Marketing for approval by the 3rd week of the prior month.
  5. If not approved by that deadline, follow up and escalate — never send unapproved EDMs.
  6. Once approved, schedule and send each EDM in Klaviyo to the correct segment.
  7. Maintain automated flows on an ongoing basis — add new products, remove discontinued ones, refresh stale content.
  8. Identify gaps in the customer journey and brief in missing flows (with proposed copy and triggers) for approval and build.
  9. Monitor open rate, click rate, revenue attribution, and flow conversion on an ongoing basis; investigate causes of notable changes and propose fixes.
  10. Every quarter, consolidate flow performance findings into a formal summary for the Head of Marketing.

Process Flow Diagram

Detailed Steps

Step 1: Plan the Monthly EDM Lineup (Campaigns)▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Ahead of each month, driven by the campaign calendar
How:
  1. Reference the campaign calendar to identify promos, launches, and always-on themes needing email support that month, per brand.
  2. Draft a proposed lineup of EDM sends for the month — roughly 8-10 for Shortcutx and 6-8 for Tradishot. Solulu and Omiee don't yet have a live website, so no EDM volume currently applies; once each brand's website launches, target 6 EDMs/month.
  3. Assign each planned EDM a tier (A/B/C/D) per the EDM Playbook, which applies brand-wide, targeting the monthly tier mix (A: 2-3, B: 1-2, C: 1-2, D: 2-3) — see Reference: EDM Tier System above.
  4. Note the intended send date and purpose (promo, launch, etc.) for each planned EDM.
Output: A draft monthly EDM lineup ready for segmentation planning.
Step 2: Define Segmentation Rules▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Alongside lineup planning
How:
  1. For each planned EDM, define the audience segment (e.g. purchase history, engagement level, brand/product interest).
  2. Document the segmentation logic clearly enough for the Head of Marketing to review alongside the drafts.
Output: Segmentation rules attached to each planned EDM in the lineup.
Step 3: Draft Individual EDMs▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: After the lineup and segmentation are set
How:
  1. Build each EDM in Klaviyo, covering subject line, copy, and visuals.
  2. Ensure content matches brand voice and the relevant campaign/promo messaging.
Output: Draft EDMs ready for review.
Step 4: Submit for Head of Marketing Approval▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: By the 3rd week of the prior month — approval must be secured before the new month begins
How:
  1. Compile the Proposed EDM Lineup, Segmentation Rules, and Individual Drafts into a Google Doc.
  2. Place the document in the shared Google Drive folder for Head of Marketing review.
  3. Follow up if not approved by the 3rd week of the prior month — do not send unapproved EDMs.
Output: An approved monthly EDM lineup, ready to schedule.
Step 5: Schedule & Send Campaign EDMs▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Once approval is received
How:
  1. Schedule each approved EDM in Klaviyo per its segmentation rule and planned send date.
  2. Confirm test sends look correct (rendering, links, personalization) before the live send.
Output: EDMs sent on schedule to the correct segments.
Step 6: Maintain Automated Flows▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Ongoing
How:
  1. Flows are always-on and triggered by customer activity (welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back/re-engagement) — distinct from the monthly campaign cadence in Steps 1-5.
  2. Add new products to relevant flows as they become available.
  3. Remove discontinued products from flow content promptly so flows never reference items no longer in production.
  4. Review each flow for stale or outdated content (expired promos, sold-out products, outdated pricing) and refresh as needed.
Output: Automated flows stay accurate, current, and free of stale content.
Step 7: Identify & Brief In Missing Flows▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Ongoing, formally reassessed each quarter
How:
  1. Identify gaps in the customer journey not yet covered by an existing automated flow (e.g. browse abandonment, replenishment reminder, review request).
  2. Draft proposed copy and trigger logic for each missing flow.
  3. Submit the proposal to the Head of Marketing for approval, following the same approval process as campaign EDMs.
  4. Once approved, build the new flow live in Klaviyo.
Output: Journey gaps identified and new flows proposed, approved, and built.
Step 8: Monitor Performance & Propose Fixes▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Ongoing — after each campaign send, and periodically for flows
How:
  1. Monitor open rate, click rate, and revenue attribution for each campaign EDM, and conversion rate for each flow, in Klaviyo.
  2. Identify likely causes for notable increases or drops (e.g. subject line, send time, segmentation, content fatigue, deliverability).
  3. Propose fixes or tests to the Head of Marketing (e.g. new subject line formula, resend to non-openers, adjusted segmentation, refreshed flow content).
Output: Ongoing performance insight, with proposed fixes actioned as agreed.
Step 9: Quarterly Automated Flow Performance Review▶
Who: Digital Marketing Manager
When: Quarterly
How:
  1. Consolidate the quarter's flow performance monitoring, content recency checks, and journey-gap findings into a single summary.
  2. Share the summary and recommendations with the Head of Marketing.
Output: A quarterly flow performance summary with suggested edits, actioned as agreed.

Exceptions and Edge Cases

A product referenced in an automated flow goes out of stock or is discontinuedRemove or update the flow content immediately — don't wait for the quarterly review.

Purpose

This process exists to ensure a consistent pipeline of high-performing video ad creative (EGC/UGC) — with static ad creative produced separately by the Brand Manager — across Meta, Google, and TikTok always-on ads, spanning the full funnel (TOFU, MOFU, BOFU), refreshed regularly to avoid fatigue and continuously improved in partnership with the Media Manager.

Scope

In Scope

  • Creating video ad creative (EGC, UGC) for Meta, Google, and TikTok always-on ads, covering TOFU, MOFU, and BOFU
  • Using AI tools (e.g. Claude, TryBloom, and other relevant tools) to support and optimize creative production
  • Soft, light-touch approval from the Head of Marketing before creative is handed off — testing is expected to happen in-platform, not gated beforehand
  • Monthly refresh of always-on creative to avoid ad fatigue, or sooner if flagged
  • Reviewing always-on ad performance in the Media Manager's Google Data Studio dashboard and consulting on what needs attention, via a weekly scheduled sync

Out of Scope

  • Media buying and budget allocation — owned by the Media Manager
  • Budget changes — require Head of Marketing approval, executed by the Media Manager
  • Performance reporting — owned by the Media Manager via Google Data Studio
  • MOFU/BOFU creative built specifically for eCommerce Campaigns (Part 4) — covered separately from always-on
  • Static ad creative production — owned by the Brand Manager, as part of Shortcutx's hyper-personalized ad strategy
